Young people in Montpellier face growing precarity: unaffordable rents, insufficient food, a mental health crisis and persistent discrimination. Nathalie Oziol wants a city that genuinely invests in its youth.

### Strong points:

- **Housing as the key to autonomy**: priority for young people in social housing allocations, support for solidarity flat-sharing and young workers' hostels.
- **Zero homeless young people**: specific shelter and support schemes for young people in crisis.
- **Access to dignified food**: solidarity grocery shops, food parcels and subsidised meals for students and precarious young people.
- **Meeting basic material needs**: assistance with transport, school supplies and digital equipment.
- **Unrestricted mobility**: free or heavily reduced public transport for young Montpellier residents.
- **Mental health as a priority**: creation of youth health centres, free first-line psychological consultations, training for frontline workers.
- **Youth health spaces**: deployment of sexual health, contraception and screening services directly in the neighbourhoods.
- **Guaranteed access to culture**: a topped-up municipal culture pass, free admission for under-18s at municipal facilities.
- **More accessible media libraries**: extended opening hours, reinforced youth activities, inclusive digital access.
- **Popular education**: support for popular education associations, youth and community centres (MJC) and neighbourhood houses.
- **Culture in all aspects of life**: artistic projects in secondary schools, high schools and informal spaces.
- **Accessible sport**: open and free facilities, supported associative coaching.
- **Rejecting the commodification of sport**: maintaining popular sporting practices outside commercial logic.
- **End of the instrumentalisation of secularism**: guaranteeing equal treatment for all beliefs in the public space.
