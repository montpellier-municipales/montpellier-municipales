Representative democracy in Montpellier is in crisis: low participation, decisions made without genuine consultation, elected officials cut off from residents. Nathalie Oziol wants a local democratic revolution, inspired by the most advanced participatory practices.

### Strong points:

- **Citizens' assembly**: creation of a randomly selected assembly, representative of Montpellier's diversity, with real deliberative power over major orientations.
- **Local citizens' initiative referendum (RIC)**: a mechanism allowing residents to propose and force a vote on any municipal issue, with a reasonable signature threshold.
- **Right to recall elected officials**: the ability for citizens to remove an elected official during their term through a petition process.
- **Citizens' conventions by sortition**: for each major issue (mobility, food, urban renewal), a deliberative citizens' convention with follow-up of recommendations.
- **Effective right of petition**: any petition reaching a minimum threshold will automatically trigger a discussion at the municipal council.
- **Voting at 16**: extending voting rights to young people aged 16 for municipal elections and local consultations.
- **Transparency of media spending**: publication of the City's communication and advertising budgets.
- **Strengthening the central town hall**: reaffirming the role of the town hall as a democratic institution in the face of private operators.
- **Neighbourhood councils with real power**: direct election of members, their own budget, right to refer matters to the municipal council.
- **Mandatory public workshops**: any major urban planning, road or infrastructure project must be subject to prior consultation with residents.
- **Exemplary conduct from elected officials**: publication of agendas, declarations of conflicts of interest, limits on the accumulation of positions and remuneration.
- **Respect for opposition rights**: guaranteed access to committees, protected speaking time, access to data and municipal services.
- **Transparent room allocation software**: end of discretionary allocations, a public and equitable digital tool.
- **Transparent allocation criteria**: association subsidies, public procurement: everything is public and justified.
- **Free display boards**: increasing citizen expression surfaces in public spaces.
