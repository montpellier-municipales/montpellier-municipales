Sexist and sexual violence, discrimination against LGBTI+ people and gender inequalities persist in public spaces, at work and in institutions. Nathalie Oziol proposes a feminist and inclusive municipal policy that goes beyond declarations of intent.

### Strong points:

- **Municipal victim reception service**: a dedicated helpdesk for victims of domestic violence, sexual violence or discrimination, operating 7 days a week.
- **Commitment alongside victims**: the City joins as a civil party to support victims of violence and discrimination in their legal proceedings.
- **Training all staff**: mandatory training on sexism, LGBTIphobia and domestic violence for all municipal personnel.
- **Discrimination liaisons in every department**: an identified contact person in each municipal directorate to handle reports.
- **Prevention and education policy**: equality education programmes, programmes on affective and sexual life, and combating stereotypes, in schools and municipal structures.
- **Exemplary public employer**: equal pay, fighting harassment, facilitating chosen part-time work, promoting women to leadership positions.
- **Access to sexual and reproductive health**: support for health centres offering abortion and contraception, information accessible to all women.
- **Emancipatory cultural visibility**: cultural programming integrating feminist and LGBTI+ representations and struggles for equality.
- **Combating workplace harassment**: clear procedures, protection for whistleblowers, effective sanctions.
- **Emergency accommodation for victims**: dedicated places for women victims of violence and LGBTI+ people in family breakdown situations.
- **Action against domestic and sexual violence**: funding for specialist associations, coordination with law enforcement and the justice system.
- **Supporting survivors in employment**: schemes to maintain or regain employment after a situation of violence.
- **Public spaces adapted for women**: lighting, secure transport, facilitated reporting of threatening behaviour.
- **Visibility of women in public spaces**: naming streets and squares after women, feminist artworks in public space.
- **Support for LGBTI+ people in crisis**: guidance towards housing, employment and care for people rejected by their families.
- **Affirming LGBTI+ rights and visibility**: support for Pride, associations and cultural events promoting LGBTI+ rights.
