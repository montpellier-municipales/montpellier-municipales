Food is a matter of public health, social justice and ecological transition. In Montpellier, too many families do not have enough to eat, while large retailers and agri-industry impose their logic. Nathalie Oziol wants a city that guarantees access to healthy and local food.

### Strong points:

- **Municipal organic community restaurants**: creation of neighbourhood canteens with a pay-what-you-can or very low price model, using organic and local produce, open to everyone.
- **Protection of agricultural land**: moratorium on artificialisation, classification of peri-urban cultivable land.
- **Solidarity agricultural land**: creation of municipal agricultural land to help farmers set up in organic agriculture.
- **Food processing tools**: vegetable preparation facilities, shared central kitchens, canning workshops available to local producers.
- **Democratic food governance**: municipal food council involving producers, consumers, associations and residents.
- **Food security**: development of social and solidarity grocery shops, food aid without stigmatisation.
- **Short supply chains and local food**: farmers' markets, direct purchasing in collective catering, support for community-supported agriculture (AMAP).
- **Plant-based collective catering**: 50% vegetarian meals in school and municipal canteens by 2028, moving towards food free of industrial animal products.
