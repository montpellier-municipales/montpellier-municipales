Population ageing is a challenge that Montpellier must prepare for ambitiously. Too many older residents live in isolation, with insufficient incomes and difficult access to healthcare. Nathalie Oziol wants a city that stands in solidarity with elderly people, guaranteeing their autonomy and dignity.

### Strong points:

- **Housing solutions for seniors**: development of non-profit senior residences, intergenerational flat-sharing and participatory housing for elderly people.
- **Guaranteed access to healthcare**: local health centres including geriatric consultations, accessible home care, transport to medical appointments covered.
- **Maintaining autonomy**: support for municipal or associative home help services, adapted assistance equipment, prevention of falls and loss of autonomy.
- **Intergenerational programmes**: projects bringing seniors and young people together in schools, youth clubs (MJC) and cultural facilities.
- **Adapted services**: home meal delivery, accessible teleassistance, day care for people with cognitive disorders.
- **Social participation**: lively social centres for seniors, neighbourhood clubs, digital workshops, cultural outings.
- **Support for carers**: recognition of the role of informal carers, access to respite arrangements, psychological support.
- **Pensions lived with dignity**: advocacy for adequate pensions, local action to reduce the cost of living for low-income retirees (social pricing of services).
- **Fighting isolation**: home visits, organised solidarity neighbourhoods, early detection of isolation by municipal services.
