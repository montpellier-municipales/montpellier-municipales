Montpellier suffers from deep health inequalities: medical deserts in working-class neighbourhoods, unaffordable out-of-pocket costs for low-income households, and insufficient prevention. Nathalie Oziol wants an ambitious public health policy rooted in the neighbourhoods.

### Strong points:

- **Municipal health centres**: creation of municipal health centres in every neighbourhood, with general practitioners, nurses and specialists accessible without upfront payment.
- **Guaranteed preventive care**: free access to screenings, vaccinations and health check-ups for everyone.
- **Access to medicines**: support for associative and cooperative pharmacies, assistance with access to medicines for low-income households.
- **Health education**: information and prevention programmes in schools, community centres and youth clubs (MJC).
- **Fighting health inequalities**: specific action in neighbourhoods where access to care is most difficult, with mobile health teams.
- **Support for vulnerable populations**: dental, ophthalmological and psychiatric care accessible to precarious people, unconditionally.
- **Dignity for health workers**: support for frontline health professionals, improved working conditions in municipal structures.
- **Mental health**: deployment of accessible psychological support services, particularly for young people and those in precarious situations.
- **Environmental health**: fighting atmospheric, noise and chemical pollution in the neighbourhoods, with concrete action plans.
- **Access to abortion and contraception**: support for family planning centres, information accessible in every neighbourhood.
