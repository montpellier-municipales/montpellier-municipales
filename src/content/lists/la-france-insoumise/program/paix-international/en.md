Cities have a role to play in international policy: they can refuse partnerships with regimes violating international law and become spaces of concrete solidarity. Nathalie Oziol proposes a decentralised cooperation policy founded on peace and human rights.

### Strong points:

- **Suspension of the partnership with Tiberias**: suspending agreements with the Israeli city of Tiberias for as long as international law is not respected in Gaza and the West Bank, making any partnership conditional on respect for human rights.
- **Montpellier: a city for peace**: adoption of a municipal charter in favour of disarmament, diplomacy and non-violent conflict resolution.
- **Revitalising existing partnerships**: strengthening cooperation with Louisville, Heidelberg, Yaoundé and other partner cities around concrete projects.
- **A popular international relations house**: transforming this space into a place for popular education on international issues, open to everyone.
- **Municipal bureau for international solidarity**: a dedicated structure to coordinate and support solidarity actions with peoples in struggle.
- **Support for solidarity associations**: sustainable funding for Montpellier associations engaged in international cooperation and humanitarian aid.
- **Celebrating world cultures**: international cultural programming, support for minority languages and the cultural expressions of communities present in Montpellier.
