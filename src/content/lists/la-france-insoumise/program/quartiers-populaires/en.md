Montpellier's working-class neighbourhoods — Mosson, Figuerolles, Celleneuve, Croix d'Argent — are too often forgotten by municipal policy, relegated to top-down renovation logic without genuine consultation. Nathalie Oziol proposes putting control back in the hands of residents.

### Strong points:

- **Local public services**: deployment of public service houses in each neighbourhood, bringing administration closer to residents.
- **Power to residents**: neighbourhood councils with real decision-making power, a participatory budget per neighbourhood, right of initiative.
- **Zero substandard housing**: action plan to eradicate unsanitary housing, with guaranteed rehousing on-site.
- **Transparent housing allocation**: independent commission, public criteria, end of clientelism.
- **Rehousing without displacement**: rejection of policies that scatter residents during rehabilitation works.
- **Truly accessible mobility**: bus lines adapted to working hours, solidarity fares, bike-share schemes in the neighbourhoods.
- **Renovated schools and educational equality**: priority investment in neighbourhood schools, class-size reduction.
- **Local and useful employment**: social insertion clauses in public procurement, creation of local jobs (mediation, cleanliness, community activities).
- **Safety through human presence**: neighbourhood concierges, mediators, appropriate lighting — prevention before repression.
- **Green and breathable spaces**: greening of school playgrounds and squares, cool islands, access to natural spaces.
- **Culture, sport and solidarity as rights**: quality sports facilities, local cultural centres, solidarity grocery shops.
- **Paillade sud**: thorough review of ongoing projects, genuine consultation with residents in the southern Mosson area.
- **Free outdoor pools in summer**: free open access to municipal swimming pools during heat waves.
