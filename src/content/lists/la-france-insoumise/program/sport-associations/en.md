Sport in Montpellier is too often reserved for those who can afford it. Between poorly maintained facilities, unaffordable club fees and underfunding of the associative movement, thousands of residents are left out. Nathalie Oziol wants a popular, emancipatory and accessible sport.

### Strong points:

- **Democratising access to sport**: solidarity pricing in all municipal sports facilities, free admission for under-18s.
- **Popular and emancipatory sport**: support for sporting practices that strengthen collective spirit, equality and health rather than commercial performance.
- **Facilities accessible to all**: bringing installations up to standard for people with disabilities, universal access guaranteed.
- **Fighting discrimination in sport**: anti-discrimination protocols in all subsidised sports associations, training for coaches.
- **Rejecting the commodification of sport**: preserving associative clubs and amateur practices from the logic of profit and privatisation.
- **Support for local sports associations**: sustainable funding, allocation of time slots and facilities, administrative support.
- **Sport and citizenship**: developing sporting projects that encourage civic engagement, social mixing and living together.
- **Democratic governance of sport**: involving clubs, athletes and residents in decisions about facilities and municipal sports policy.
- **Health sport**: free adapted sport programmes for seniors, people with disabilities and groups far removed from sporting practice.
- **Support for associative life**: simplification of procedures, access to premises, multi-year funding for associations of general interest.
