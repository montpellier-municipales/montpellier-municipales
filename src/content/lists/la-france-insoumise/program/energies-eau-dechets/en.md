Water, energy and waste management are common goods that market logic has too often privatised or squandered. Nathalie Oziol wants a city that takes back control of these vital resources and accelerates the transition to a sober and public model.

### Strong points:

- **Municipal energy utility**: creation of a municipal company for the production and distribution of renewable electricity (solar, wind), with solidarity pricing.
- **Energy efficiency in public buildings**: multi-year renovation plan for all municipal buildings, with a target of carbon neutrality by 2035.
- **Phasing out fossil fuels**: gradual abandonment of gas and oil in municipal assets, replaced by clean energy.
- **Urban heating networks**: development of biomass boilers and geothermal energy to supply the neighbourhoods.
- **Citizens' convention on waste**: participatory process to co-construct a policy of source reduction and improved sorting.
- **Source reduction**: support for repair, reuse and resource recovery centres; fighting food waste in collective catering.
- **Waste education**: school and community programmes on reduction, composting and sorting.
- **Recycling of construction materials**: local sector for the recovery of rubble and deconstruction materials.
- **Guaranteed access to water**: no cuts for households in difficulty, progressive pricing with the first portions free.
- **Rainwater harvesting**: public equipment and assistance for individuals to collect and use rainwater.
