Security in Montpellier does not boil down to police presence or video surveillance. Nathalie Oziol proposes an approach founded on prevention, human presence and the involvement of residents — real safety, not a security-state approach.

### Strong points:

- **Prevention before repression**: redirecting resources towards social prevention, education and mediation rather than punitive measures.
- **Local mediation**: deployment of mediators in the neighbourhoods, active during the day and in the evening to prevent conflicts before they erupt.
- **Reoriented municipal police**: refocusing municipal police missions towards prevention, relations with residents and support for victims — less repression, more useful presence.
- **Neighbourhood concierges**: redeploying security budgets towards posts of human presence in working-class neighbourhoods (building caretakers, mediation workers).
- **Fighting police discrimination**: training of officers in professional ethics, accessible appeals procedure, citizen oversight.
- **Residents as actors in their own safety**: regular consultation with residents to jointly define the security priorities of their neighbourhood.
- **Community approaches**: support for neighbourhood initiatives, citizen patrols and listening spaces run by associations.
