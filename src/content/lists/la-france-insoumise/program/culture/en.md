Culture in Montpellier is too often reserved for an elite or instrumentalised for institutional communication purposes. Nathalie Oziol wants an ambitious and democratic cultural policy that supports creation and guarantees access for all.

### Strong points:

- **Culture as a public service**: rejection of market logic in access to culture, sustainable public funding for local cultural structures.
- **Democratising access**: solidarity pricing in all municipal cultural facilities, free admission for under-18s and RSA recipients.
- **Support for artistic creation**: artist residencies, maker spaces, support for independent companies and local artists.
- **Valuing heritage and memory**: maintenance and enhancement of Montpellier's heritage, recognition of popular and colonial memories.
- **Support for intermediate cultural venues**: youth and community centres (MJC), associative concert halls, art-house cinemas, independent bookshops — active support against closures.
- **Fighting artist precarity**: raising fees, respecting social rights, refusing unpaid performances in public structures.
- **Arts education in schools**: school-company partnerships, artists in residence in educational establishments.
- **Festivals and popular events**: support for accessible festivals, rooted in the neighbourhoods, with genuine social mixing.
- **Universal cultural accessibility**: sign language interpretation (LSF), audio description, disabled access in all cultural facilities.
