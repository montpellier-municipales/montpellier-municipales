People with disabilities face daily obstacles in Montpellier: inaccessible public spaces, unsuitable housing, difficulty accessing employment, and too often invisibility in public policy. Nathalie Oziol wants a city where disability is taken into account in every decision.

### Strong points:

- **Accessibility in all policies**: the principle of universal accessibility integrated from the design stage of any municipal facility, service or public space.
- **Fighting discrimination**: reporting and sanctioning procedures for disability-related discrimination in public services and the associative sector.
- **Adapted housing**: development of housing adapted to different types of disability, assistance with adapting existing homes.
- **Access to employment**: insertion clauses for disabled people in public procurement, support for sheltered workshops (ESAT) and adapted enterprises.
- **Specialist services**: strengthening home support services, adapted transport and specialist reception structures.
- **Inclusive citizen participation**: ensuring that people with disabilities can fully participate in local democratic bodies.
- **Inclusive education**: reinforced support for teaching assistants (AESH), school accessibility, refusal of involuntary part-time schooling.
- **Digital accessibility**: all municipal digital tools compatible with assistive technologies.
- **Accessible culture and sport**: accessible cultural and sports facilities, adapted programmes, solidarity pricing.
- **Disability resource house**: a space for resources, guidance and support for people with disabilities and their families.
