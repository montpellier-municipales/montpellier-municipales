Montpellier continues to artificialise its agricultural land and concrete over its outskirts, to the detriment of residents and living ecosystems. Faced with the climate emergency and a crisis of meaning, Nathalie Oziol proposes a democratic, sober and ecological approach to urban planning.

### Strong points:

- **Urban planning based on real needs**: rejection of large, unnecessary and imposed projects, prioritising local amenities.
- **No substandard housing in building permits**: minimum quality conditions required for all new construction.
- **Calming traffic**: reduction of motor traffic by neighbourhood, traffic-calmed streets and genuine low-emission zones.
- **Accessibility of public spaces**: ensuring that every resident, regardless of age or disability, can move freely around the city.
- **Dignified and non-hostile public spaces**: removing anti-homeless street furniture, designing welcoming spaces for all.
- **Fighting light pollution**: reducing unnecessary night-time lighting, protecting biodiversity and residents' sleep.
- **Human-scale urban planning**: limiting the height and density of buildings in residential areas, preserving the existing urban fabric.
- **Reclaiming parks and public squares**: greening, fountains, shade, free convivial spaces.
- **Urban cooling**: fighting heat islands through greening, permeable surfaces and misters.
- **Strengthened environmental compensation**: any destruction of vegetation compensated on a like-for-like basis.
- **Adapting schools to the climate**: greened school playgrounds, sun protection, available fresh water.
- **Desealing the soil**: reducing concreted surfaces, promoting natural water infiltration.
- **Protection of agricultural land**: moratorium on any new project that artificialises cultivable land.
- **Urban democracy**: mandatory public workshops for any major project, with residents' veto rights.
