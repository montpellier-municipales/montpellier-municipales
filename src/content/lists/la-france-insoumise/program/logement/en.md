In Montpellier, the housing crisis is a daily reality: speculative rents, substandard housing, evictions, and a market dominated by the interests of landlords and tourist rental platforms. Nathalie Oziol proposes making housing an effective right, managed by public authorities.

### Strong points:

- **Municipal housing rights office**: creation of a one-stop shop to guide residents through their procedures and defend their rights against landlords and lessors.
- **Protection of tenants**: fighting abuse, unlawful evictions and slum landlords; legal action in the name of the municipality.
- **Requisitioning vacant housing**: requisition properties that have been empty for more than two years to return them to the social housing circuit.
- **Fighting speculation and tourist rentals**: strict regulation of Airbnb-type lettings, taxation of secondary residences, rent caps.
- **Production of affordable housing**: massively relaunching the construction of social and very social housing, prioritising households on waiting lists.
- **Municipal guarantees**: setting up rent guarantees for low-income households, facilitating access without a bank deposit.
- **Popular home ownership**: supporting housing cooperatives and social homeownership schemes.
- **Emergency accommodation**: strengthening reception capacity for homeless people and victims of violence.
- **Thermal renovation**: ambitious plan for energy rehabilitation of social and private housing stock, with assistance for low-income homeowners.
- **Universal accessibility**: bringing housing and communal areas up to standard for people with disabilities.
- **Resident participation**: restoring genuine consultation in housing allocations and rehabilitation projects.
