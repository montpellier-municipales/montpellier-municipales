Animals, wild or domestic, are part of the living world that the city must protect. In Montpellier, animal welfare policies have too often remained symbolic. Nathalie Oziol wants a genuine public policy in favour of animal welfare.

### Strong points:

- **Clear political responsibility**: designating an elected official explicitly in charge of animal issues, with a dedicated budget.
- **Maintaining hunting bans**: preserving and strengthening the ban on hunting in communal spaces.
- **Ban on animals in circuses**: maintaining and strictly enforcing the ban on circuses with wild animals.
- **Transparency of public action**: annual publication of a report on the City's animal policies.
- **Protection and care**: agreements with animal protection associations, funding of care for stray animals.
- **Sterilisation of stray cats**: systematic sterilisation programme and management of urban cat colonies.
- **Wildlife refuge spaces**: creation and maintenance of refuge zones, hedgerows, ponds and meadows in public parks.
- **Prevention of abandonment and fighting mistreatment**: awareness campaigns, facilitated reporting, strengthened sanctions.
- **A city designed for cohabitation with living beings**: taking ecological corridors into account in urban planning, reducing obstacles for wildlife.
- **End of lethal methods**: banning lethal methods of animal management where alternatives exist.
- **Training of municipal staff**: all staff in contact with the public trained in animal protection and ethical management.
- **Youth education**: integration of respect for living beings into municipal educational programmes.
- **Facilitating daily life with animals**: dog parks, water points, premises that welcome pets.
- **Committed public procurement**: introducing animal welfare criteria in public food procurement contracts.
- **50% reduction in animal products by 2032**: a progressive target in municipal collective catering.
