Public education in Montpellier suffers from underinvestment, class closures and persistent school segregation. Nathalie Oziol wants an ambitious educational policy that places equality at the heart of municipal priorities.

### Strong points:

- **Free school supplies**: the City covers all school materials for every primary school pupil.
- **Breakfast at school**: introducing a morning welcome with breakfast in all municipal schools.
- **Free school meals**: extending free school canteen meals, starting with the most low-income families.
- **School gardens**: developing educational vegetable gardens in each school, linked to canteen food supply.
- **Fighting school segregation**: an active policy of social mixing in school zoning and structures.
- **Tackling school dropout**: early identification and individualised support for children in difficulty.
- **Zero class closures**: committing the City alongside teaching teams to maintain all posts and classes.
- **Ecological and free meals**: canteens with 80% organic and local produce, weekly vegetarian meals, progressive free access.
- **Ending precarity for municipal staff**: permanent contracts for ATSEM and cleaning staff, improved working conditions.
- **Stable teaching teams**: advocacy to end the constant movement of teachers in priority education zones (REP).
- **Involving staff**: co-constructing educational policies with frontline teams.
- **Preventing educational violence**: training for staff, clear protocols, spaces for children to speak up.
- **Public early childhood**: developing municipal crèches and drop-in childcare, reducing waiting lists.
- **Supported parental leave**: concrete assistance for families during a child's early years.
- **Outdoor learning**: outdoor classes, regular nature outings, connection to the living world from the youngest age.
- **Green school trips for all**: municipal funding so every child can go on a green school trip at no extra cost.
- **Screen-free weeks**: annual experimentation with disconnected weeks, with cultural and sporting alternatives.
