Montpellier is a city where the car often remains the only option due to a lack of real alternatives, penalising the most precarious and accelerating the climate crisis. Nathalie Oziol proposes a mobility revolution, founded on public transport, cycling and free access.

### Strong points:

- **Moratorium on unnecessary road projects**: suspension of any new road infrastructure project not justified by service needs.
- **Massively reinforced public transport**: increased frequencies, extended operating hours, new lines in underserved areas.
- **Networks adapted to real needs**: consultation with residents to rethink routes and timetables according to daily usage patterns.
- **New transport funding**: advocacy for an increased mobility levy and a contribution from large companies.
- **Cycling as a pillar of mobility**: a secure and continuous cycling network across the whole city, secure parking, cargo bikes available for hire.
- **Universal accessibility**: bringing all stops, vehicles and platforms up to standard for people with disabilities.
- **Territorial coordination**: articulating the Tam, Hérault Transport and SNCF networks for smooth connections.
- **Moving towards free travel**: full free public transport for under-18s and the most low-income residents, with progressive free access for all.
- **Road sharing campaign**: education on cohabitation between pedestrians, cyclists and motorists.
- **Shared electric vehicles**: development of electric car-sharing fleets in underserved neighbourhoods.
- **Citizens' convention on the car**: public debate on the place of the automobile in Montpellier by 2030.
- **Buses in underserved areas**: extension of lines to peripheral communes and neighbourhoods not currently served.
- **Dignified stations and bus terminals**: rehabilitation of bus terminals and improvement of waiting areas.
