Racism and discrimination persist in Montpellier as across France: in access to housing, to employment, in police checks, and sometimes within the public services themselves. Nathalie Oziol wants a city that is fully committed to fighting all forms of discrimination.

### Strong points:

- **End of the instrumentalisation of secularism**: not using the principle of secularism to target religious practices or discriminate against residents, particularly veiled women.
- **Joining as a civil party**: engaging the City as a civil party in judicial proceedings concerning racist acts targeting Montpellier residents.
- **Training all public staff**: mandatory and regular training for all municipal staff on discrimination, professional ethics and equal treatment.
- **Exemplary municipal public service**: zero tolerance for discriminatory behaviour in City services, accessible reporting procedures.
- **Discrimination observatory**: an independent body to monitor discrimination in Montpellier, with annual publication of a public report.
- **Protection of precarious foreigners**: support for undocumented people and asylum seekers, guaranteed access to essential services without any residency condition.
- **Reception and support mechanisms**: creation of municipal guidance structures for discrimination victims.
- **Municipal education and prevention policy**: programmes in schools, cultural spaces and associations to deconstruct prejudice and promote living together.
- **Rights of Travellers**: guarantee of dignified and functional reception areas, end of arbitrary evictions.
- **Memories of the anti-racist struggle**: recognition and transmission of the historical struggles against colonialism and racism; support for places of memory.
- **French language learning**: literacy and French as a foreign language programmes, accessible and free for all residents.
- **Colonial history institute**: creation of a research and mediation space on the colonial history of Montpellier and France.
