Montpellier's economy is marked by glaring inequalities: ubiquitous large retailers, a weakened local commerce sector, and rising precarious employment. Nathalie Oziol wants a local economy rooted in the neighbourhoods, respectful of workers and in ecological transition.

### Strong points:

- **Blocking large retailers**: moratorium on any new large commercial development or extension, priority for local shops.
- **Local commerce serving the neighbourhoods**: support for the establishment of essential shops in commercial deserts, controlled rents via public land ownership.
- **Metropolitan plan "10,000 sustainable jobs"**: launch of a collaborative employment plan with local economic actors, targeting ecological transition sectors.
- **Control of commercial land**: acquisition of strategic commercial premises to prevent speculation and maintain a diversity of activities.
- **Municipal productive business hubs**: shared spaces for craftspeople, micro-enterprises and social economy (ESS) structures, at accessible rents.
- **Public procurement as a lever**: systematic social and environmental clauses in public contracts, priority for local companies and the social economy.
- **Support for ecological conversion**: technical and financial support for local businesses undertaking their transition.
- **Cooperative and participatory economy**: support for worker cooperatives (SCOP, SCIC), housing cooperatives and democratically governed enterprises.
- **Municipal employment services**: a one-stop shop for guidance, training and return to employment, in liaison with local professional sectors.
- **Women's insertion**: specific schemes for the employment of women who are victims of violence or experiencing professional breakdown.
- **Municipal craft councils**: representation of craftspeople in local economic decision-making bodies.
- **Fighting precarious work**: municipal action against involuntary part-time work, undeclared labour and low wages in public procurement.
- **Cooperative restaurants**: support for associative and cooperative canteens offering meals at fair prices.
- **Municipal economic observatory**: public monitoring of local economic data, transparency on subsidies granted to businesses.
- **Renegotiation of the budgetary framework**: action on toxic loans and disadvantageous contracts to free up financial room for manoeuvre.
