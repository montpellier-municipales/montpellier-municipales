Michaël Delafosse commits to strengthening local services that ease daily life for Montpellier residents and protect their purchasing power.

### Key points:

1. **Purchasing Power Office**: Creation of a single municipal contact point to help residents access grants, social rates, and existing support mechanisms (water, energy, transport, food).
2. **Expanded Social Pricing**: Extension of social pricing across municipal services (swimming pools, school catering, cultural and sports activities).
3. **Maintained Free Public Transport**: Continuation of fully free TAM public transport for all metropolis residents.
4. **Social Grocery Stores and Food Access**: Support for food aid organizations and development of access to quality food for low-income households.
5. **Neighbourhood Services Preserved**: No closures of district town halls or proximity services; strengthening of community centres.
