**Kadija Zbairi** is a **licensed architect (DPLG)**, originally from Montpellier. After working in other cities, she returned to her home territory in 2017 with a renewed perspective on the urban, social, and environmental challenges facing the metropolis. She specialises in **biomimetic architecture**, a discipline that draws inspiration from living systems to design more resilient, resource-efficient urban spaces in harmony with local ecosystems. She was the only architect to contribute to the **2019 Assises de la transition agro-écologique et de l'alimentation durable**, demonstrating her commitment at the intersection of urban planning, ecological stewardship, and food sovereignty.

Engaged in public life, Kadija Zbairi stood as a candidate in the **legislative elections**, asserting a citizen presence in political life without party affiliation. Her approach is rooted in a **non-partisan citizen movement**, convinced that the transformation of the city must come from residents themselves, not from party logic.

For the 2026 municipal elections, Kadija Zbairi leads the list **"La Municipaliste"**, whose slogan — *"À vous de jouer !"* ("Your turn!") — encapsulates a political vision grounded in **active citizen participation**. The programme is built around four main axes: the city as commons, culture in everyday life, ecological transition, and citizen participation. A candidacy that seeks to place residents at the heart of the decisions that shape the city.
