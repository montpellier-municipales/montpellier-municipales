### Judicial Context

Mohed Altrad was **convicted in December 2022** by the Paris Criminal Court in the context of what is generally called the *Laporte-Altrad affair*. In this case, he was found guilty of **active corruption, influence peddling, and misuse of corporate assets**; the court handed him an **18-month suspended prison sentence**, a **fine of 50,000 euros**, as well as an **additional penalty of ineligibility**.

This conviction, from a first instance, is **being appealed**; the procedure is still ongoing, with a Court of Appeal hearing scheduled (or postponed) to a later date.

Electorally, the mentioned conviction **does not systematically prevent the publication of a candidacy** if appeals are pending or if legal conditions for ineligibility evolve with judicial decisions. In France, the effects of an additional penalty of ineligibility can depend notably on its period of application, its confirmation on appeal, and the starting point of its legal duration.
