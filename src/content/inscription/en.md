# Registering to Vote in Montpellier

To participate in the municipal elections on **March 15 and 22, 2026** in Montpellier, registration on the electoral roll is mandatory. This page outlines the eligibility requirements and the steps to take to exercise your right to vote.

---

## Eligibility Requirements

Voting rights in municipal elections are open to individuals meeting the following criteria:

### For French Citizens

*   Be **18 years of age or older** by the day before the election;
*   Enjoy full **civil and political rights**;
*   Be registered on the municipality's **electoral roll**.

### For European Union Citizens

Nationals of another European Union member state may vote in municipal elections provided they:

*   Are **18 years of age or older**;
*   Reside in Montpellier;
*   Are registered on the **supplementary electoral roll**.

*Note: Non-EU citizens cannot participate in municipal elections in France.*

---

## How to Register

Registration is free and can be completed in three ways.

### 1. Online Registration

The fastest method is via the **Service-Public.fr** online service. You will need to provide digital versions of the following documents:
*   A valid identity document;
*   Proof of address less than 3 months old.

### 2. Registration at City Hall

You can visit Montpellier City Hall or one of the local district offices. You must present the originals and copies of your identity document and proof of address.

### 3. Registration by Mail

It is possible to send the CERFA registration form (available online), accompanied by photocopies of the supporting documents, to the City Hall's elections department.

---

## Calendar and Deadlines

For the 2026 municipal elections:

*   First round: **Sunday, March 15, 2026**
*   Second round: **Sunday, March 22, 2026**

The deadline to register on the electoral roll is **Friday, February 6, 2026**. Any application received after this date cannot be processed for this election.

---

It is recommended to check your registration well in advance of the vote, especially if you have recently moved (even within the same city).

**Moving:** Address changes are not automatic. If you have moved to a different municipality or changed your address within Montpellier, you must report this change by submitting a new registration request.
